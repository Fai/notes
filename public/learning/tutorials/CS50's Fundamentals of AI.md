On the agenda:
- **Lecture 0: Playing** - Exploring how AI can play games: Minimax, Monte Carlo Tree Search, reinforcement learning
- **Lecture 1: Predicting** - Exploring how AI can make predictions based on data: regression, classification, neural networks
- **Lecture 2: Analyzing** - Exploring how AI can find patterns in data: clustering, association rule learning, recommender systems
- **Lecture 3: Sensing** - Exploring how AI can interpret sensory data like images and sounds: edge detection, convolutional neural networks, deep learning
- **Lecture 4: Communicating** - Exploring how AI can process and respond to natural language: embeddings, attention, transformers
- **Lecture 5: Generating** - Exploring how AI can generate text, images, and sounds: retrieval-augmented generation, autoencoders, diffusion models
- **Lecture 6: Moving** - Exploring how AI can interact with the physical world: motion planning, localization and mapping, object detection
