## Screening Assessors

### Sensory Panel Selection

- Threshold Sensitivity
    - Detection - Lowest concentration perceptible
    - Recognition - Lowest concentration for identification
    - Difference - Concentration change necessary for perception of change
        - Reduce cost
    - Terminal - Saturation concentration, no perception of incremental change
- Intensity Ranking
- Bias Selection
    - Association Effect - The mind tries to relate the stimulus perception to the previous experience, when the same sensation was encountered.

### Sensory Panel Effectiveness

- Flavor wheel
    - The larger the gap, the more difference in sensory they are
    - Going from broader categories to smaller categories
- Sensory Lexicon
- Reference vs Standard
    - Standard - perfectly consistent, pharmaceutical grade tablet of sensory
    - Reference - reasonably consistent, manufactured product readily available

### Q&A

How often should we calibrate

> Before you use the tools or panels, calibrate. Even if not using, calibrate regularly (6 months ~ 2years intervals)
> 

What kind of sensory perception drop with your aging

> Taste perception (Threshold) and Aroma (Detection threshold and Recognition, the recognition one can be recover by training)
> 

What we can do to prevent the loss of sensory perception

> Avoid smoking and vaping, Avoid putting something too hot into your month and take care of your health
> 

How do we know if our sensory perception aren‚Äôt up to the point anymore?

> The company will tell us, if you pass the calibration, you passed, if not, you might need more training or consider other option.

### **Shelf-life**

- **Best by** date - **Quality** statement
    - Set up the set on the shelf and fresh roast and can see the difference
    - Sensory statistics table and see the difference
- **Use by** date - **Safety** statement
    - Coffee are not going to be dangerous, it‚Äôs going to stale.
        - Depends on how old, may get oily and woody.
        - Stale is Oxidized Coffee - Staling report from SCA
- Roasted on date, if not provided the best by date, are left the decision up to the consumer.

### Cupping definition

Fragrance = Dry

Sniff and Describe, One round around the table since the coffee ground are time sensitive 

Water = 93 +-

Steel 3-5 min

Stir and break

Aroma = Wet

Slurp at three different temperatures

8~9.5 min after pour, check by touching to your lip

Hot 160f/70c - focus first on flavor and aftertaste ! Be careful not to burn your tongue - aerate to make the flavor get to the retro nasal 

- flavor
- aftertaste
    - How long
    - Quality

Warm 140f/60c - acidity, body, balance

Coke 100f/40c - defects, off-flavors

- uniformity - same enough (commercial significance) -> off-flavor
- clean cup - is it defect
- sweetness - can I perceive sweetness in this coffee
